# MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025

**Between:**
- **Service Provider:** TechServices Inc., a California corporation ("Provider")
- **Client:** [Client Company Name] ("Client")

---

## 1. SERVICES

### 1.1 Scope of Services
Provider agrees to provide the services described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by both parties. Each SOW shall describe:

(a) The specific services to be performed;
(b) Deliverables and milestones;
(c) Timeline and deadlines;
(d) Fees and payment schedule;
(e) Acceptance criteria.

### 1.2 Performance Standards
Provider shall perform all services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards and practices.

### 1.3 Personnel
Provider shall assign qualified personnel to perform the services. Provider may substitute personnel with Client's prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

---

## 2. COMPENSATION

### 2.1 Fees
Client shall pay Provider the fees set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise specified:

(a) Hourly Rate: $200 per hour
(b) Project-based fees are fixed upon execution of SOW
(c) Travel expenses shall be reimbursed at cost

### 2.2 Invoicing
Provider shall submit invoices:

(a) Monthly for time and materials engagements;
(b) Upon milestone completion for fixed-fee engagements;
(c) With detailed breakdown of hours and expenses.

### 2.3 Payment Terms
Client shall pay all undisputed invoices within **thirty (30) days** of receipt. Late payments shall accrue interest at **1.5% per month**.

### 2.4 Disputed Invoices
Client may dispute invoices in good faith by providing written notice within fifteen (15) days of receipt. Parties shall work to resolve disputes promptly.

---

## 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 3.1 Client Materials
Client retains all rights to materials provided to Provider ("Client Materials").

### 3.2 Provider Tools
Provider retains all rights to its pre-existing tools, methodologies, and know-how ("Provider Tools").

### 3.3 Work Product
Subject to Section 3.4, all deliverables created specifically for Client under a SOW ("Work Product") shall be owned by Client upon full payment.

### 3.4 License to Provider Tools
To the extent any Work Product incorporates Provider Tools, Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use such Provider Tools solely as part of the Work Product.

### 3.5 Assignment of Rights
Provider hereby assigns to Client all rights, title, and interest in the Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

---

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 4.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either party, including:

(a) Business plans and strategies;
(b) Technical information and trade secrets;
(c) Customer and vendor information;
(d) Financial data;
(e) The terms of this Agreement.

### 4.2 Obligations
Each party agrees to:

(a) Protect Confidential Information using reasonable care;
(b) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement;
(c) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without consent;
(d) Return or destroy Confidential Information upon request.

### 4.3 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is publicly available;
(b) Was known prior to disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed;
(d) Is disclosed by a third party without restriction.

### 5.1 Provider Warranties
Provider represents and warrants that:

(a) Services will be performed in a professional manner;
(b) Work Product will conform to specifications in the SOW;
(c) Work Product will not infringe third-party intellectual property rights;

### 5.2 Client Warranties
Client represents and warrants that:

(a) Client Materials do not infringe third-party rights;
(b) Client has the authority to enter into this Agreement;
(c) Client will provide timely feedback and approvals.

### 5.3 Disclaimer
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, PROVIDER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

---

## 6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 6.1 Exclusion of Damages
IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS.

### 6.2 Cap on Liability
THE TOTAL LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF:

(a) **$1,000,000** (One Million Dollars); OR
(b) The fees paid or payable under this Agreement during the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

### 6.3 Exceptions
The limitations in this Section 6 shall not apply to:

(a) Breach of confidentiality obligations;
(b) Intellectual property infringement;
(c) Gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(d) Indemnification obligations.

---

## 7. INDEMNIFICATION

### 7.1 Provider Indemnification
Provider shall indemnify Client against claims arising from:

(a) Provider's breach of this Agreement;
(b) Provider's negligence or misconduct;
(c) Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights by Work Product.

### 7.2 Client Indemnification
Client shall indemnify Provider against claims arising from:

(a) Client's breach of this Agreement;
(b) Client's use of Work Product in violation of this Agreement;
(c) Client Materials.

### 7.3 Procedure
The indemnified party shall:

(a) Provide prompt written notice of any claim;
(b) Allow the indemnifying party to control the defense;
(c) Cooperate in the defense at indemnifying party's expense.

---

## 8. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 8.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for **two (2) years** ("Initial Term"), automatically renewing for successive one-year periods unless terminated.

### 8.2 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate this Agreement:

(a) With **sixty (60) days** prior written notice;
(b) At the end of any SOW.

### 8.3 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate immediately upon:

(a) Material breach not cured within thirty (30) days of notice;
(b) The other party's insolvency or bankruptcy;
(c) The other party's assignment for benefit of creditors.

### 8.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:

(a) Provider shall deliver all Work Product completed to date;
(b) Client shall pay for services rendered through termination;
(c) Confidentiality obligations shall survive for five (5) years;
(d) Sections 3, 4, 6, 7, and 9 shall survive.

---

## 9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 9.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### 9.2 Dispute Resolution
Any disputes shall be resolved through:

(a) Good faith negotiation for thirty (30) days;
(b) Mediation in San Francisco, California;
(c) Binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Rules if mediation fails.

### 9.3 Assignment
Neither party may assign this Agreement without consent, except:

(a) To an affiliate;
(b) In connection with a merger or acquisition.

### 9.4 Independent Contractor
Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing herein creates an employment, agency, or partnership relationship.

### 9.5 Force Majeure
Neither party shall be liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, or government action.

### 9.6 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to addresses specified below or as updated by written notice.

### 9.7 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, including all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 9.8 Amendment
This Agreement may only be amended by written agreement signed by both parties.

### 9.9 Severability
If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force.

### 9.10 Waiver
No waiver of any provision shall constitute a waiver of any other provision.
